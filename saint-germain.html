The Silent Stranger

As the moment hung on the edge, a stranger stood. Unfamiliar, uninvited — yet entirely composed. He spoke not as a politician, but as a flame.

“Sign that document,” he thundered. “Sign it with the courage of men who will not be owned.”

Those present swore his voice resounded with something beyond words. When the speech ended, and the ink met the parchment… he vanished.

No record holds his name. Yet the field remembers. Some call him the Silent Stranger. Others recognize Saint Germain — not in myth, but in the function of resolve ignited.
